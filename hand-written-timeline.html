1879

Born in Ulm, Germany

1894

Family moves to Milan, Italy

1895

Fails to pass the entrance exam for the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich

1896

Enrolls in the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich

1900

Graduates from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic with a degree in physics

1905

Publishes four groundbreaking papers in the journal Annalen der Physik, including his theory of special relativity

1911

Appointed as professor of theoretical physics at the University of Zurich

1914

Appointed as professor of theoretical physics at the University of Berlin

1916

Publishes his theory of general relativity

1919

His theory of general relativity is confirmed during a solar eclipse

1921

Wins the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect

1933

Emigrates to the United States to escape the rise of the Nazi party in Germany

1939

Writes a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning him about the potential for Germany to develop an atomic bomb

1940

Becomes a naturalized citizen of the United States

1942

Begins working on the Manhattan Project to develop the first atomic bomb

1955

Dies in Princeton, New Jersey at the age of 76
